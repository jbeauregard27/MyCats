Hi there!

I'm Midnight, a sleek and mysterious six-year-old black cat. My humans say I'm quite the looker, though I think my true charm lies in my personality. I have something called, eosinophilic granuloma complex, which sounds fancy but mostly means I have to wear this annoying cone. Ugh, I hate it! In my house, I'm not alone. I have a few feline siblings: Sunshine, Evie, and Momo. Sunshine and I are siblings by blood, and she's got this golden fur that matches her name. She's a bit of a diva and sometimes picks on Evie, who’s more on the timid side. Evie tries to steer clear of Sunshine, but she’s sweet and gentle when we do cross paths. Momo, on the other hand, is a playful ball of energy, always trying to get us all involved in some new game or chase. One of my favorite memories is the time we all conspired to steal treats. It was Momo’s idea, of course. She spotted where the humans kept the treats and got Sunshine and me in on the plan. Evie was too scared to join in, but she watched from a safe distance. Momo created a diversion by knocking over a vase (don’t worry, it was already cracked), while Sunshine and I made a beeline for the treat cupboard. We managed to get it open and had a feast until the humans caught us. They couldn’t stay mad for long—we’re too adorable! Nap time is sacred in our house, especially for me. When I nap with my humans, they take off my cone, and it’s pure bliss. Sunshine often curls up at the foot of the bed, while Momo sprawls out wherever she pleases. Evie usually finds a quiet corner where she can feel safe. We may have our little squabbles, but at the end of the day, we’re a family. And for me, any day without the cone is a good day, especially if it includes bird-watching and a nice, long nap.
